My Projects
Family Recipes

A recipe site my brother asked me to make for our mom for christmas 2023. She still uses the original hand-written recipe cards and he wanted to incoroporate that sentimentality, while creating a lasting record of all the recipes handed down through the generations. It would also be a quick and easy way for her to find recipes she wants to make.

CRONbook

A simplified 'facebook' based on my housing group from college. Our name was Sigma Phi Omicron but we called ourselves 'CRONS' for short, hence 'CRONbook'. This project was daunting at first, but when the back-end came together is when I started becoming confident that I can really be a developer.

Chess

A terminal based chess game written in ruby. This was one of my favorite projects because of the increasingly intricate classes needed to represent the pieces, and the sense of accomplishment when it finally came together. Unfortunately, I am still not very good at actually playing chess...

My Story

It's been a winding journey to arrive at web development. I have always loved logic and problem-solving, which led me to pursue mathematics in college. But I was always drawn to people, which led me to pursue teaching and eventually ministry, after college. However, I reached a point where I needed to step away from that line of work, and began searching for a new career.

Right away, my wife and other friends said I should look into coding. I was hesitant at first, but one day I took their advice and discovered something that made sense for me. In coding, logical thinking and problem-solving worked together with a person-centered motivation. I was hooked.

Luckily, a friend of mine introduced me to The Odin Project, and I dove right in. Working through courses and projects on my own time while working odd-jobs to pay bills.

I have learned a lot on the journey, not the least of which being how to google things I don't know. Before coding I worked at a bowling alley, amazon warehouse, a church, senior at home care, and even did the gig economy thing as a personal shopper. While I gained a lot from those experiences, none of them made sense for me as a life career. With development, I believe I've found that.
